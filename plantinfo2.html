Plant Propagation in Water

Check soil moisture: Before watering, stick your finger about an inch or two into the soil. If it feels dry, it's time to water. If it's still moist, skip watering. Water thoroughly: When you do water, do so deeply so all the roots get a drink. Water until you see a small amount of water draining from the bottom, and then empty the drainage tray so the plant isn't sitting in excess water. Ensure proper drainage: Always use pots with drainage holes to prevent overwatering and allow roots to get air.

Provide the right light: Research your specific plant's needs, as some prefer bright, indirect light while others need more direct sun. Low-light plants still need some light, such as what a bathroom window provides. Move seasonally: Adjust plant placement as the seasons change, especially in winter when there are fewer daylight hours. Consider grow lights: If you don't have enough natural light, a grow light can help supplement it.
